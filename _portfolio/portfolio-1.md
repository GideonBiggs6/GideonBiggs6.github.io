excerpt: "The project is a Theatrical Mic Plot and Input Logistics Matrix for a high school production of High School Musical. It serves as a master technical blueprint designed to optimize a limited inventory of wireless microphones for a large ensemble cast of actors. The system tracks every character's stage presence scene-by-scene to ensure seamless audio mixing and coordinate rapid, foolproof physical microphone swaps backstage and in the greenroom. To build the foundation of the project, I utilized past mic lists to build data grids that highlighted microphones on scenes that they were needed. For console preparation, I configured channel names, color-coded layouts, and digital mute groups. I calculated precise logistical timing to ensure backstage crew members had enough physical time to execute microphone changes. Finally, I applied live audio engineering principles like gain staging to balance different vocal dynamics, while designing physical labelling systems to keep the backstage hardware organized. Through this project, I learned that a successful live sound mix relies heavily on meticulous pre-production data rather than just real-time intuition. I gained a deep, practical understanding of radio frequency behavior, discovering how physical building materials and hundreds of audience cell phones can alter wireless signals. Most importantly, I learned how to integrate technical requirements with human variables, realizing that backstage tracking sheets must be designed around the high-stress environment of actors undergoing rapid costume changes. The production required prominent vocal features for over thirty performers in need of mics, but the theater only owned sixteen wireless microphone packs. I solved this resource bottleneck by engineering a shared-mic matrix that paired ensemble members who were never on stage at the same time, maximizing hardware efficiency without sacrificing audio quality.
